DR. Norman Borlaug

The man who saved a billion lives

DR.Norman Borlaug, trains biologist in Mexico on how to increase wheat yields.

HERE TIME-LINE OF DR.NORMAN BORLAUG

  • 1914-Born in Cresco,Lowa
  • 1933-Leaves his Family Farm to attend University of Minnesota.

  • 1935-Has to stop school and save more money. Works in the civilian conservation corps,helping starving American "I saw how food change them", he said "all of this left scar on me"

  • 1937-finishes university and takes a job in the US forestry service.

  • 1970-recieves the Noble Prize
  • 1983-Helps seven African countries dramatically increase their maize and sorghum yields
  • 1984-Becomes a distinguish professor at Texas A&B University

  • 2005-states"we will have to double the world food supply by 2050." Argues that genetically modified crops are the only way we can meet the demand, as we run out arable lands.Says that GM crops are not inherently dangerous because "we've beeen genetically modifying plants and animals for a long time.Long before we call it science,people were selecting the best breeds."

  • 2009-Dies at the age of 95.
